Ask them, and the pronoun refers to the polytheists of Mecca or to the children of Adam. { أَهُمْ أَشَدُّ خَلْقاً أَم مَّنْ خَلَقْنَا } Meaning what has been mentioned of the angels, the heavens, the earth, and what is between them, the stars, and the shooting stars. { مِنْ } For the triumph of the wise, and it is indicated by its generality and its coming after that. The reading of those who read "أم من عددنا" (or from what we have counted), and His saying: { إِنَّا خَلَقْنَـٰهُم مّن طِينٍ لاَّزِبٍ } (Indeed, We created them from sticky clay) is the distinction between them and it, not between them and those before them like 'Aad and Thamud. The intention is to affirm the afterlife and refute its impossibility. The matter in it is to add to them and to those before them equally. Its confirmation is that the impossibility of that is either due to the lack of suitability of the material, and their original material is the sticky clay resulting from the combination of the watery part with the earthy part, and both remain capable of combining again. They knew that the first human was born from it, either by their admission of the creation of the world or by the story of Adam. They witnessed the birth of many animals from it without intermediary or contact. Therefore, they must allow their return in the same way. Or it is due to the lack of ability of the doer, and whoever has the power to create these things has the power to do what is not counted with them, especially since He created them first, and His power is inherent and does not change. { بَلْ عَجِبْتَ } From the power of Allah, the Exalted, and their denial of the resurrection. { وَيَسْخُرُونَ } They mock your amazement and your affirmation of the resurrection. Hamza and Al-Kisa'i read it with a damma on the ta', meaning the utmost of My power and the multitude of My creations, that you are amazed by them. These, in their ignorance, mock it. Or you are amazed that those whose deeds are these deny the resurrection, and they mock those who affirm it. Amazement at Allah, the Exalted, is either by assumption and imagination or by the meaning of magnification that is necessary for Him, for it is a wonder that overtakes a person when they magnify something. It is said that it is expressed by saying, meaning: He said, O Muhammad, indeed you are amazed. { وَإِذَا ذُكّرُواْ لاَ يَذْكُرُونَ } When they are reminded, they do not remember, or when they are admonished with something, they do not take heed. Or when they are reminded of what indicates the truth of the resurrection, they do not benefit from it due to their ignorance and lack of thought. { وَإِذَا رَأَوْاْ ءايَةً } A miracle that indicates the truthfulness of the one who speaks of it. { يَسْتَسْخِرُونَ } They exaggerate in mockery and say that it is magic, or some of them call upon others to mock it. { وَقَالُواْ إِن هَـٰذَا } They mean what they see. { إِلاَّ سِحْرٌ مُّبِينٌ } Clearly magic. { أَءِذَا مِتْنَا وَكُنَّا تُرَاباً وَعِظَـٰماً أَءِنَّا لَمَبْعُوثُونَ } Its origin is "are we to be resurrected when we die," but they replaced the verbal form with the nominal form and advanced the adverb and repeated the hamza for emphasis in denial, and to indicate that the resurrection is astonishing in itself and in this state, it is even more astonishing. Therefore, it is more eloquent than the reading of Ibn 'Amir by omitting the first hamza and the reading of Nafi' and Al-Kisa'i and Ya'qub by omitting the second. { أَوَ ءَابَاؤُنَا ٱلأَوَّلُونَ } A parenthetical phrase in the place of { إِن } and its noun, or in the place of the pronoun in "مبعوثون" (resurrected), for it is separated from it by the hamza of interrogation to increase the exclusion due to the remoteness of their time. Nafi' paused, and according to the narration of Qalun, Ibn 'Amir, and the waw is for repetition. { قُلْ نَعَمْ وَأَنتُمْ دٰخِرُونَ } Humiliated, and He sufficed with it in the response due to the precedence of what indicates its permissibility and the occurrence of the miracle on the truthfulness of the informant about its occurrence. It was read "قال" (he said), meaning Allah or the Messenger, and Al-Kisa'i alone read "نِعْمَ" (indeed) with a kasra, and it is a language in it. { فَإِنَّمَا هِيَ زَجْرَةٌ وٰحِدَةٌ } The response to an implied condition, meaning if that is the case, then the resurrection is { زَجْرَةٌ } a single shout, and it is the second blowing of the horn, like the shout of the shepherd to his sheep when he calls them and commands them to return, like the command { كُنْ } (Be) in creation, and therefore it is arranged after it. { فَإِذَا هُمْ يَنظُرُونَ } Then they will be standing from their beds alive, looking, or waiting to see what will be done with them. { وَقَالُواْ يٰوَيْلَنَا هَـٰذَا يَوْمُ ٱلدّينِ } The day on which we will be judged for our deeds, and their speech has been completed with it. His saying: { هَـٰذَا يَوْمُ ٱلْفَصْلِ ٱلَّذِى كُنتُمْ بِهِ تُكَذّبُونَ } is the response of the angels, and it is said that it is also from the speech of some of them to others. The decision is the judgment, or the distinction between the doer of good and the doer of evil. { ٱحْشُرُواْ ٱلَّذِينَ ظَلَمُواْ } The command of Allah to the angels, or the command of some of them to others to gather the wrongdoers from their place to the standing. It is said from it to Hellfire. { وَأَزْوٰجُهُمْ } And their likes, the worshipper of the idol with the worshipper of the idol, and the worshipper of the star with its worshipper, like His saying, the Exalted: { وَكُنتُمْ أَزْوَاجاً ثَلَـٰثَةً } [Al-Waqi'ah: 7]. Or their wives who are on their religion, or their peers from the devils. { وَمَا كَانُواْ يَعْبُدُونَ مِن دُونِ ٱللَّهِ } From the idols and other than them, an addition to their humiliation and disgrace, and it is general, specified by His saying, the Exalted: { إِنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ سَبَقَتْ لَهُم مّنَّا ٱلْحُسْنَىٰ } the verse, and in it is evidence that { ٱلَّذِينَ ظَلَمُواْ } are the polytheists. { فَٱهْدُوهُمْ إِلَىٰ صِرٰطِ ٱلْجَحِيمِ } So guide them to a path for them to follow
